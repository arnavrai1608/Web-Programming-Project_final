  • QUIZ
  1. Each team has 11 players, including a captain and wicketkeeper.
  2. The objective is to score more runs than the opposing team.
  3. An over consists of 6 legal deliveries by the bowler.
  4. A batsman can be dismissed by being bowled, caught, LBW, run-out, or stumped.
  5. Boundary rules: 4 runs for hitting the ball to the boundary on the ground, 6 runs if it clears the boundary.
  6. Powerplay restricts fielders outside the inner circle.
  7. No-ball and wide-ball penalties grant extra runs to the batting side.
  8. Free Hit is awarded after a no-ball, allowing the batsman to play without risk of dismissal (except run-out).
  9. The fielding team must appeal to the umpire for a dismissal decision.
  10. A tied match in limited-overs can be resolved using a Super Over.
